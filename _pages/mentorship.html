Guiding the next generation of researchers in solving real-world challenges through data and mathematics.

I mentor students and young researchers in Applied Mathematics and Statistics, Biomathematics, and Data Analysis, helping them build strong research, analytical, and problem-solving skills for real-world applications in public health and related fields. My mentorship covers areas such as mathematical modelling of infectious diseases, biostatistics and data analysis, public health research, and academic writing. I also provide guidance and supervision for research projects, thesis and dissertation support, assistance with data analysis and interpretation, and support in developing research proposals and publications.
