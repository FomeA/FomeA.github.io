MAT 102 – Ordinary Differential Equations

Course Description

Ordinary Differential Equations introduces students to the formulation and solution of differential equations arising from a wide variety of physical and real-world problems. The course covers first and second order differential equations, Laplace transforms, and qualitative analysis of differential systems. It equips students with both theoretical understanding and practical techniques for solving differential equations and applying them in scientific contexts.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

Knowledge-Based Outcomes

  • Understand how ordinary differential equations arise from real-world problems.
  • Explain the theory behind different types of differential equations.
  • Describe methods for solving first and second order differential equations.

Skill-Based Outcomes

  • Solve various types of ordinary differential equations.
  • Apply Laplace transforms in solving differential equations.
  • Analyze qualitative behavior of differential systems.

Course Content

  • Topic I: Introduction to Differential Equations
  • Topic II: First Order Differential Equations
  • Topic III: Second Order Linear Differential Equations
  • Topic IV: Laplace Transforms
  • Topic V: Qualitative Theory of Differential Equations

Mode of Delivery

Lectures, seminars, and case studies.
