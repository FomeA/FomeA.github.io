MAT 102: Ordinary Differential Equations

Contact Hours: 3 per week (2 hrs lecture, 1 hr seminar)

Programme: BAED I (Mathematics Option)

Course Type: Compulsory

Course Aim

This course is designed to lead students to the formulation of ordinary differential equations from a wide variety of physical phenomena. It also enables students to understand the theory and practice of solving various types of ordinary differential equations.

Learning Outcomes

  • Show how ordinary differential equations arise from a wide variety of problems.
  • Demonstrate understanding of the theory and practice of solving various types of differential equations.

Course Content

Topic 1: Introduction

  • Definition of a Differential Equation
  • Basic Mathematical Models
  • Classification: Ordinary vs Partial Differential Equations
  • Order, Degree, and Linearity
  • Solutions of Differential Equations

Topic 2: First Order Differential Equations

  • Linear First Order Equations
  • Separable Equations
  • Homogeneous Equations
  • Bernoulli Equations
  • Exact Equations & Integrating Factors
  • Initial Value Problems
  • Existence and Uniqueness Theorem
  • Applications

Topic 3: Second Order Linear Differential Equations

  • General Theory
  • Existence and Uniqueness
  • Linear Independence
  • Fundamental Solutions
  • Homogeneous and Non-Homogeneous Equations
  • Undetermined Coefficients
  • Variation of Parameters
  • Initial & Boundary Value Problems
  • Applications

Topic 4: Laplace Transforms

  • Definition of Laplace Transform
  • Transforms of Standard Functions
  • Properties and Theorems
  • Inverse Laplace Transform
  • Partial Fractions
  • Transform of Derivatives
  • Applications to Differential Equations

Topic 5: Qualitative Theory

  • Stability of Linear Systems
  • Equilibrium Solutions
  • Phase Plane Analysis
  • Applications (Predator-Prey Models)

Assessment Methods

Lectures, Seminars, and Case Studies.
