QMS 101 – Introductory Statistics

Course Description

Introductory Statistics introduces students to fundamental statistical concepts and methods used in analyzing data in business, industry, and national contexts. The course covers data collection techniques, presentation of data, measures of central tendency and dispersion, as well as basic probability and probability distributions. It equips students with the skills to interpret data, apply statistical reasoning, and support decision-making using quantitative analysis.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Knowledge-Based Outcomes
    • Understand fundamental statistical concepts and terminology.
    • Explain methods of data collection and presentation.
    • Describe measures of central tendency, dispersion, and basic probability principles.
  2. Skill-Based Outcomes
    • Apply statistical techniques to analyze real-world data.
    • Interpret results from statistical calculations and tests.
    • Use statistical reasoning to support effective decision-making.
