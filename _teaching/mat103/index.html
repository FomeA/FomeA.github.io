MAT 103 – Numerical Analysis I

Course Description

This course introduces students to ordinary differential equations arising from a wide variety of physical and real-world phenomena. It develops both theoretical understanding and practical techniques for solving different types of differential equations, with applications in science and engineering.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Knowledge-Based Outcomes
    • Explain how ordinary differential equations arise from real-world problems.
    • Understand key concepts such as order, linearity, and classification of equations.
    • Describe analytical methods for solving differential equations.
  2. Skill-Based Outcomes
    • Solve first and second order differential equations using appropriate methods.
    • Apply Laplace transforms to solve differential equations.
    • Interpret solutions in applied contexts.

Course Content

  • Topic I: Introduction to Differential Equations
  • Topic II: First Order Differential Equations
  • Topic III: Second Order Linear Differential Equations
  • Topic IV: Laplace Transforms
  • Topic V: Qualitative Theory of Differential Equations

Assessment

Lectures, seminars, and case studies.
