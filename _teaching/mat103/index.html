MAT 103 – Numerical Analysis I

Course Description

This course introduces students to numerical techniques for solving mathematical problems that cannot be solved analytically. It develops methods for approximation, error analysis, root finding, interpolation, numerical integration, and solving systems of linear equations. Students are also introduced to computational tools such as MATLAB and MAPLE.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Knowledge-Based Outcomes
    • Understand the difference between numerical and analytical methods.
    • Explain sources and types of errors in numerical computation.
    • Describe numerical techniques for solving mathematical problems.
  2. Skill-Based Outcomes
    • Derive and apply numerical methods for solving equations and approximations.
    • Implement interpolation, integration, and linear system-solving techniques.
    • Use MATLAB or MAPLE for computational problem solving.

Course Content

  • Topic I: Basic Concepts of Numerical Methods
  • Topic II: Errors in Numerical Computation
  • Topic III: Solutions of Nonlinear Equations
  • Topic IV: Polynomial Interpolation and Approximation
  • Topic V: Numerical Integration
  • Topic VI: Systems of Linear Equations

Assessment

  • Formative Assessments (Ongoing monitoring).
  • Summative Assessments (End-of-course evaluations).

Mode of Delivery

Lectures, seminars, and computer-based practical sessions.
